Intell-Cornell Cup Competition

The Tufts Robotics team is building a robotic arm intended for use with any mobile robot. Trinity Fire-Fighting Competition 2015

The Tufts Robotics team is participating once again, in the Trinity firefighting robot contest. This year we are sending two teams. My role in this has been both as an instructor, and a team member. I have helped direct the solidworks design for both teams. I also have aided in the design, construction, and programming of my teams robot. This year, we hope to improve upon the performance of our robot, and do even better than last year. We also hope to submit a new design, via a group of newer robotics team members, who we are guiding through the process.

MakeMIT

The idea was to make a prototype for a security device or tracker for personal belongings. The intention of the idea is for safe stowing of bags in public places, and the prevention of theft. A combination of an accelerometer, GPS tracker, alarm, and mobile alert platform would offer a fairly complete security, and recovery package.The extent of it's connectivity, and range would be an interesting cost/benefit analysis problem, but I thought it would be good project to hack. I chose the KinomaJS platform because, it came prepackaged with a lot of functionality that definitely sped up our prototyping process.

Trinity Fire-fighting Competition 2014

As part of the Tufts Robotics team I helped in the design, construction, and programming of this robot. The goal was to successfully, and autonomously, navigate a small maze of "rooms", find a candle and extinguish it. We placed in the top 15, took 1st in the olympiad challenge, and got the smallest robot prize.(awarded to the smallest bot to successfully complete the 3 trials)
